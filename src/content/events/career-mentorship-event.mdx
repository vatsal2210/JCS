
# Jain Community Services of Canada Hosts Milestone Event

Jain Community Services (JCS) of Canada marked a momentous occasion on Saturday, May 3rd, 2025, by hosting a vibrant in-person event at the Jain Society of Toronto (JSOT), Moti Champsee Cultural Centre, bringing together over 100 members of the Jain community for a morning of celebration, learning, and connection.

## Lifetime Achievement Award

The highlight of the event was presenting the Lifetime Achievement Award to Manharbhai and Sunitaben Sheth, in recognition of their extraordinary 40 years of humanitarian service to the Jain community in Canada. The award was a heartfelt tribute to their lifelong dedication to Jain values, community building, and service.

> "This honor is not just a reflection of our personal journey, but a tribute to the spirit of seva, unity, and resilience that defines our community."

## Career Guidance and Mentorship Session

The event included an interactive career development session featuring:

- Expert-led workshops on career planning and professional development
- One-on-one mentorship opportunities with industry leaders
- Networking sessions with professionals from various fields
- Resources and guidance for career advancement

## Website Launch

Another key highlight of the day was the official launch of the new JCS Canada website. This digital platform is set to serve as a comprehensive resource for Jain community members across Canada, offering information on services, programs, and opportunities for involvement.

## Community Celebration

To close the event, attendees enjoyed a delicious Jain meal prepared by the JSOT Kitchen Team, whose culinary efforts reflected the community's emphasis on purity, hospitality, and shared joy.

The event was made possible by the dedicated efforts of the Jain Community Services (JCS) Board Members, Sheth family members, volunteers, and supporters. The generous support and use of the hall and facilities provided by Jain Society of Toronto (JSOT) played a key role in the event's success.

*Due to space limitations, we were only able to accommodate only 100 attendees, and regretfully, many Jain community members and well wishers could not be included.*
